### Postmortem lessons

![TGDF 2](/assets/media/migrated/blog/tgdf__2-ff8418241e-w576.webp)

-   What went right
    1.  Found money and people: co-developed with a game company and received publisher funding.
    2.  Started moving before everything was settled: tested and iterated early while the plan was still incomplete.
    3.  Target:[Uncle mobile games](http://blog.monkeypotion.net/legiondary/legiondary-close-beta)
        -   Lower competition
        -   Casual rhythm: play when you want, stop when you want
        -   Emotional appeal
    4.  Took breaks at the right time.
    5.  Talk about success after it actually happens.

-   What went wrong
    1.  People problems: collaboration creates differences between people. The three barriers to entrepreneurship are money, people, and law.
    2.  Schedule delays: a game is not only its core loop. Polish, operations tuning, social features, and similar work all consume time.
    3.  Both mainstream and independent:
        -   Japanese style VS. American style
        -   Cute girls vs. beast men
        -   Auto Play VS. Manual Play
        -   Disposable story VS. real story content
        -   Operations VS. Experience
        -   Gashapon VS. Paid Download
    4.  Testing started too late.
    5.  The project was not settled early enough, and the publisher contract was terminated.
